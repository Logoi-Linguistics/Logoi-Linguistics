; Radical! But if all this is the Lisp, then where's the Prolog?

english
"Socrates is a man."
"The plural of man is men."
"All men are mortal."
"Is Socrates mortal?"

; That's not Prolog, that's just logic!

prolog
"'{1} is a {2}.'('Socrates', 'man').
"'The {1} of {2} is {3}.'('plural', 'man', 'men').
"'All {1} are {2}.'('men', 'mortal').
'All {1} are {2}.'(X, 'mortal'),
'The {1} of {2} is {3}.'('plural, Y, X),
'{1} is a {2}.'('Socrates', Y)."

; Ahh, there's the Prolog! On second thought... the English version is easier to read.

"'{1} is a {2}.'('Cain', 'man')."
"'{1} is a {2}.'('Abel', 'man')."
"'{1} is a {2} of {3}.'('Eve', 'parent', 'Cain')."
"'{1} is a {2} of {3}.'('Eve', 'parent', 'Abel')."
"'{1} is a brother of {2}'(A, B) :-
A \= B,
'{1} is a {2}.'(A, 'man'),
'{1} is a {2}.'(B, 'man'),
'{1} is a {2} of {3}.'(C, 'parent', A),
'{1} is a {2} of {3}.'(C, 'parent', B)."
"'{1} is a brother of {2}.'('Cain', 'Abel')."
